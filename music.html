Current Projects

Wild Eye

Wild Eye strikes a zen-like balance between the psychedelic rock and folk sounds of past and present. Turn up your headphones to find a fingerpicked guitar magically fitting alongside hushed mantras and face-melting guitar solos. The binding element is Ross Williams, who I Heart Local Music proclaimed to have “One of the prettiest voices on the scene”. His skillful modern production is the final alchemical ingredient that makes Wild Eye a rare pleasure: an artist who’s painstakingly crafted a singular voice to push the art and expand the mind.

Wild Eye’s performances span from Austin, Texas as part of the Midcoast Takeover, to opening for national touring acts such as The Spirit of the Beehive, as well as sharing bills with local favorites like Calvin Arsenia, Mysterious Clouds, Akkilles, and more.

Past Projects

No Cave

I was looking for a drummer for a different band (This Evil) and Nick (drums) came over to jam, and somewhere in that session my roommate James came by and asked if he could play bass. Our chemistry was powerful and immediate! It was clear that we had to start a new band with this lineup. And so No Cave began.

James and I had been jamming often and were heavily inspired by the krautrock of 70's Germany. We wanted to bring that sense of open experimentation to the new group. Nick came from a background in math rock and midwest emo, bringing that intense energy to our spaced-out jams. From our formation we spent a lot of our time improvising and piecing together songs- the main method used in the composition style of our favorite bands like Can, Harmonia, and Neu. We eventually had enough material to start playing live, and audiences responded well to our blend of styles.

Tropic of Leo

The songwriting project of Joel Stratton, I played bass in the live band. I also recorded, produced, mixed, and mastered the eponymous Tropic of Leo EP. Very fun and challenging pop music to play! I remember Joel was very inspired by Big Star as well as modern one man band's like Tame Impala (Kevin Parker) and Mac Demarco at the time. This is probably my best work playing bass.
