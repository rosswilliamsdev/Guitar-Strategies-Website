Humble Beginnings & Formal Training

I began my journey as a musician when I was gifted guitar lessons on my 15th birthday. I had no special gift for music, and being the only musician in my family, I had no real exposure to music beyond the mainstream hits of the time. I grew up on 90’s pop country (Alan Jackson, Garth Brooks, Shania Twain, etc), rock, and pop music. For the first year or so of lessons, I practiced some but didn’t take it too seriously. The height of my interest at the time was learning the guitar parts to my favorite pop-punk songs.

Then, in my senior year of high school, my best friend (a gifted pianist) told me he was auditioning for a spot at a prestigious music school. At the time I was transitioning out of playing sports year-round and wasn’t sure what I wanted to do for college (I am also a first-generation college student). I looked up the audition requirements for the music school and it finally clicked for me: do these things well, and you’re in. For the next year, I basically locked myself in my room to study those audition requirements: scales, sight reading, ear training, classical guitar, and a performance piece. With help from my guitar teacher, I passed the audition! However, my friend and I both decided not to go as it was prohibitively expensive, even with a small scholarship. I opted to accept a full-ride scholarship at a community college closer to home.

From there I was swept into the college experience of music: jazz combos, big bands, choir, madrigals, voice lessons, piano classes, music history classes, performances, clinics, traveling, and more. I had my share of formative experiences and plenty of failures too, but I’m grateful for all the friends and mentors I made along the way. I started my first band based on my own original music with friends I made in jazz band around 2010. Ever since then, we have been playing in each other’s projects in one form or another.
